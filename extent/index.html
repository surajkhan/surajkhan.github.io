An overview of spatial extent

The human auditory system has an admirable ability of sensing sounds which have an audible
impression of being spatially extended in space. Perceived horizontal and vertical width of an auditory event is  called spatial extent. It assists in rough description of location and size of sound objects. The perception of sound enveloping us, as if we were sitting in a live concert or orchestra, is something we all enjoy and desire.

However, the perception of spatial extent is crucial for visually impaired people, as it helps them to gain a sense of auditory space.

The exact methodology and technique to synthesize the perception of spatial extent is confidential as of yet, because of its commercial viability. Once the technique becomes common, this section will be updated detailing the technique that can be used for synthesizing a varying degree of spatial extent.
